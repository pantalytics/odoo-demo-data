# Bean Forge BV

**Legal name:** Bean Forge BV
**HQ:** Utrecht, Netherlands
**Founded:** 1987
**Headcount:** 25
**Exports:** 80%+ of revenue outside NL
**Ownership:** Third-generation family business

## What they build

Precision drum coffee roasters for specialty coffee companies worldwide. Stainless steel workhorses with Italian burners, German electronics, Dutch obsession. Four product lines:

- **Forge Atelier** — 1kg sample roaster (QC labs, training, micro-roasters) — ~€18k
- **Forge Studio** — 15kg flagship workhorse — ~€65k
- **Forge Foundry** — 60kg production roaster — ~€180k
- **Forge Control** — SaaS roast-profile software, subscription — ~€150/mo per machine

Plus accessories, afterburners, chaff collectors, service parts.

## Origin story

Founded in 1987 by **Teun Boon Sr.**, son of a Dutch green-coffee importer. After a decade watching his father's roasting customers wrestle with inconsistent equipment, he declared the commercial roasters on the market "roasted like ovens, not instruments" and set out to build something worthy of the beans.

First machine was welded in a rented workshop in Utrecht. First customer was a small roastery in Antwerp that still runs a restored 1989 Forge on the showroom floor.

Today the founder's grandchild, **Teun Boon** (third generation), runs the company. Same obsession, same workshop building (now expanded to include a second hall), same Friday-afternoon calibration roast ritual.

## The hook

> *"If Bram hasn't tasted the first roast, it doesn't ship."*

Every machine that leaves the factory is calibration-roasted by **Chief Engineer Bram "The Nose" Visser** before the crate is nailed shut. Each roaster is signed by the technician who built it. There's a wall of Polaroids in the breakroom — every customer café, lab, and roastery that runs a Forge, sent in by the buyers themselves.

It's not a marketing gimmick. It started because an early customer complained about a faulty probe in 1993, and Bram's predecessor instituted the rule. It stuck.
