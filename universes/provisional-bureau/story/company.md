# The Provisional Bureau

**Legal name:** The Provisional Bureau B.V.
**HQ:** Amsterdam, Netherlands (Westerpark — converted gasworks building)
**Founded:** 2021
**Headcount:** 12
**Revenue mix:** ~55% project work, ~35% retainer, ~10% workshops & advisory
**Ownership:** Three founding partners (Hugo Maartens, Sam Quist, Indra Vermeulen)

## What they do

Brand strategy, identity, and digital — for established organizations that have outgrown their current brand and want to reinvent without losing what made them themselves. Heritage brands, scale-ups past Series B, museums, NGOs, the occasional regulated industry (banks, insurers, energy) trying to look less stodgy.

Four service lines:

- **Strategy** — brand strategy sprints, naming, narrative, positioning.
- **Identity** — visual identity systems, design systems, brand guidelines.
- **Digital** — boutique websites, design-system implementation, brand-led product UI.
- **Stewardship** — monthly retainers to keep a brand consistent and evolving across channels (subscription product).

## The hook

> *"We don't do logos. We do legacies."*

Three guesses who wrote that. (It was Hugo, in the first week.) The team rolls its eyes at it but the line works in pitches, so it stays on the website.

The actual hook — the one the work delivers on — is that The Provisional Bureau treats a rebrand as a year-long arc, not a six-week deliverable. They take fewer clients, charge more, stay longer. Half their revenue is from the third year of the same client relationship.

## Origin story

Hugo Maartens left a large London-based brand consultancy in 2020 after a decade of "watching strategy decks die in PowerPoint hell." He moved back to Amsterdam, called Sam Quist (an ex-colleague, creative director, also tired of bigness), and Indra Vermeulen (a friend from university who'd been running new-business at a big agency). They opened in February 2021 in a Westerpark co-working office with three desks and one client — a Dutch heritage cheese brand that needed a younger story.

By end of 2021 they had four clients and had hired Margot Bakker as "office manager and emergency adult." By 2024 they had twelve people and had moved to their own floor in the same gasworks building.

The name "The Provisional Bureau" was supposed to be a placeholder while they figured out a real name. They never figured out a real name.

## Brand voice (their own)

- Considered, never breathless.
- Editorial register — the tone of a thoughtful magazine, not an agency reel.
- Self-aware enough to wink occasionally; never glib.
- Sentences are allowed to be short.
